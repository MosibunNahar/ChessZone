Bangladesh Opening by Mujahedul Anwar
The opening controls four central squares. The rapid growth of pawns make the opening more strong. It is easy to open the pieces even if the opponent start acttack or defends. This opening is for long cassel lovers. This opening is for 500 - 900 rated players.

It starts with (F) file pawn to f4. First, a pawn is moved to f4 to take control of the center and help other pieces develop. Then, a knight comes out to a good spot on f3. The bishop goes to g2, which is a special move called "fianchetto," making it strong along a long diagonal. The d4 pawn is pushed forward, making a strong stand in the middle.

More pieces come out: the e3 pawn and the bishop to d2. Then a knight goes to e2, followed by the queen moving to e2. Finally, the king takes a step to the side, called castling, to keep safe. These moves set up the game for White, giving them a solid position and a plan for future moves.

Italian game by Zawata by Zawata
Italian game is for white
Italian game begins from 1)e4,e5 2)knight f3,knight c6 3)bishop c4.The point of this is to control the center quickly with pawn and knight and put bishop into its most dangerous square.By doing this we can castle safely and reduces the chances of checking to the king.

French defence by Zawata
French defence

This is for black. French defence starts from 1)e4,e6 2)d4,d5.This is one of the first strategic openings that every chess player should learn.After e5 both sides will have pawn chains.One risk of this opening is that the c8-bishop can be very hard to develop.

Sicilian defense by Zawata
Sicilian defense

Sicilian defense Sicilian defense starts from 1)e4,c5.This is the most popular choice of aggressive players with the black pieces.Often white will play 3)Knight f3 4)d4 which will gain central space but it allows black to benefit by exchanging a central pawn with a bishop’s pawn.
